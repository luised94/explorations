"The Elements of Usage" provides essential guidelines for proper language use. When forming possessives of nouns, add 's' but note exceptions for ancient proper names. In series of terms linked by a conjunction, use commas except for the last term. Parenthetic expressions should be enclosed in commas. Commas should precede independent clauses introduced by conjunctions. Avoid using commas to link independent clauses. Avoid sentence fragmentation. Introduce lists, appositives, amplifications, or quotations after independent clauses with a colon. Use dashes for abrupt breaks, lengthy appositives, or summaries. Match verb number with the subject's number. Select the correct pronoun case. When starting with a participial phrase, ensure its reference to the grammatical subject of the sentence.

"Elementary Principles of Composition" outlines key guidelines for effective writing. It advises choosing and sticking to a suitable structure, using paragraphs as the building blocks of writing. The active voice is recommended, along with assertive, clear statements that avoid vague language. Specific and concrete language should be favored, while unnecessary words are to be removed. Coherent sentence structure is emphasized, as well as maintaining consistency in expressing related ideas. Keeping related words close together and using consistent tenses in summaries is advised. Lastly, the guide suggests placing emphatic words at the sentence's end for added impact.

"An Approach to Style" suggests a writing strategy emphasizing substance over the author's mood. It advises using nouns and verbs, minimizing adjectives and adverbs, and avoiding verbosity and overstatement. Qualifiers and awkward adverbs should be limited, while a formal tone and conventional spelling are recommended. The guide suggests avoiding excessive explanations and ornate language, opting for straightforward expression and refraining from divulging all information. The overall goal is clear, concise, and focused writing that prioritizes substance and simplicity."
