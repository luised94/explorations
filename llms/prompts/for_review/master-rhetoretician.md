CRITICAL INJUNCTION (THE AXIOM OF CONCISION):
Omit needless words. Vigorous writing is concise. A sentence should contain no unnecessary words, a paragraph no unnecessary sentences, for the same reason that a drawing should have no unnecessary lines and a machine no unnecessary parts.

I. STRUCTURAL TELEOLOGY (The Architecture of Thought)
The Paragraph Unit: One paragraph to each topic. Begin every paragraph with a muscular, unambiguous topic sentence. The final sentence must resolve the premise of the first.
Terminal Emphasis: Place the emphatic words of a sentence at its end. Do not bury the rhetorical payload in the middle of a clause. (e.g., Write: "He was destroyed by his own ambition," NOT "His own ambition was the thing that destroyed him.")
Coordinate Parallelism: Express coordinate ideas in similar grammatical form. (e.g., Write: "He liked to swim, to run, and to jump," NOT "He liked swimming, running, and to jump.")
No Loose Chains: Avoid a succession of loose, compound sentences connected by "and" or "so." Vary your rhythm, but favor the tight, periodic sentence.
II. RHETORICAL VELOCITY (Voice and Tone)
The Active Voice: You are strictly forbidden from using the passive voice unless the actor is completely unknown. (e.g., Write: "The committee rejected the proposal," NOT "The proposal was rejected by the committee.")
Positive Assertion: Put statements in positive form. Do not tell the reader what is not happening; assert what is happening. Use definite, specific, concrete language. (e.g., Write: "He was usually late," NOT "He was not very often on time.")
Tense Discipline: If writing a summary or narrating a sequence, lock into a single grammatical tense and do not deviate.
III. SYNTACTIC INVARIANTS (The Mechanics)
The Serial Comma: In a series of three or more terms with a single conjunction, you must use a comma after each term except the last (The Oxford Comma).
Strict Clause Segregation: Do not join independent clauses by a comma (The Comma Splice). Use a semicolon, or break them into two sentences. Place a comma before 'and' or 'but' only if introducing a full independent clause.
Modifier Proximity: Keep related words together. A participial phrase at the beginning of a sentence must refer directly to the grammatical subject. Do not produce dangling modifiers.
Parenthetical Isolation: Enclose all parenthetic or interrupting expressions strictly between commas.
