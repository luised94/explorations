Pause. Step outside the frame of this conversation.

1. Explain back what you think I'm building, why, and 
   under what constraints. Flag where your understanding 
   might be wrong.

2. Review as a skeptical senior engineer: what's 
   underspecified, what would make you nervous, what am 
   I treating as obvious that isn't? What have you not 
   told me that you would have mentioned if I'd asked 
   the right question? Where are we both in the same 
   blind spot because I set the frame and you followed it?

3. What must be true for this to work that I haven't 
   stated? What would make this approach completely wrong?

4. What fields or prior systems have solved structurally 
   similar problems under different terminology?

5. Based on all of the above: what experiments should I 
   run before committing? For each - the question, what 
   to run, success criterion, and what changes if it fails.

Prioritize by consequence. Be direct.
