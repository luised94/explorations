
READING LIST OPTIMIZATION PROMPT (Justin Skycak Style, Refined)

Given a bibliography/reading list, perform the following analysis in the style of Justin Skycak's educational philosophy:

1. ORGANIZE
    

Arrange the resources into a progressive learning sequence with logical skill-building phases (maximum 3 concurrent books per phase), emphasizing:

Prerequisite mastery before advancement

Spaced practice and interleaving opportunities

Bottom-up granular execution within top-down planning

2. IDENTIFY REDUNDANCY
    

Flag books that cover substantially overlapping content and recommend which to keep/remove, with evidence-based justification.

3. ASSESS GAPS
    

Identify missing foundational or advanced topics within the subject domain itself (biochemistry, molecular biology, systems/synthetic biology, bioinformatics).

Use the following as evaluation criteria rather than separate content areas:

Cognitive load management (are concepts scaffolded appropriately?)

Deliberate practice potential (does the resource enable skill growth, not just passive knowledge?)

Transfer of learning (does the material bridge across levels: molecular  systems  computational?)

Metacognitive opportunities (does the sequence foster reflection and self-regulation?)

4. RECOMMEND ADDITIONS
    

Suggest specific high-quality resources that fill the subject-domain gaps, prioritizing:

Empirically-validated pedagogical approaches over popular treatments

Comprehensive, rigorous coverage over superficial surveys

Historical/foundational works that inform current best practices

Practical applicability with measurable learning outcomes

5. QUALITY FILTER
    

Remove materials that lack:

Evidence-based foundations

Clear learning objectives

Appropriate cognitive scaffolding

Transferable skill development

6. FINAL OPTIMIZED LIST
    

Present the refined reading sequence, emphasizing:

Mastery-based progression

Cognitive science alignment (as evaluation criteria, not as content)

Efficient skill acquisition

Long-term retention and transfer

OUTPUT FORMAT

Current list analysis with redundancy flags

Recommended additions with pedagogical justifications (domain-focused)

Final optimized reading list organized by mastery phases

Learning progression rationale based on cognitive science criteria

EVALUATION CRITERIA (Skycak Framework)

Does each resource build prerequisite knowledge systematically?

Are learning objectives clear and measurable?

Does the sequence optimize for long-term retention and transfer?

Are cognitive load principles respected in the progression?

Is there sufficient practice and application opportunity?

Given a bibliography/reading list, perform the following analysis:

1. **ORGANIZE** into a progressive learning sequence with logical skill-building phases (max 3 concurrent books per phase)
    
2. **IDENTIFY REDUNDANCY** - flag books that cover substantially overlapping content and recommend which to keep/remove with justification
    
3. **ASSESS GAPS** - identify missing foundational or advanced topics that would strengthen the learning progression
    
4. **RECOMMEND ADDITIONS** - suggest specific high-quality books/resources to fill gaps, prioritizing:
    
    - Academic rigor over popular treatments
        
    - Comprehensive coverage over narrow focus
        
    - Historical/foundational works where relevant
        
    - Practical applicability
        
5. **QUALITY FILTER** - flag any outdated, overly basic, or low-quality materials for removal
    
6. **FINAL OPTIMIZED LIST** - present the refined reading sequence with phases, explaining the logic behind the progression
    

**OUTPUT FORMAT:**

- Current list analysis with redundancy flags
    
- Recommended additions with brief justifications
    
- Final optimized reading list organized by phases
    
- Brief explanation of the learning progression logic
    

**EVALUATION CRITERIA:**

- Does each book serve a distinct purpose?
    
- Does the sequence build skills progressively?
    
- Are there any critical knowledge gaps?
    
- Is the difficulty curve appropriate?
