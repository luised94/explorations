Recommendation: what comes after D-UI-1
Your own roadmap model already answers most of this, and I'd lean on it rather than override it. The model's sequencing puts modularization (#1) next, then the study curriculum (#3), then SM2 (#6) - but only after adaptive selection (#7). Here's how your candidate ideas map onto that, grouped by what they actually depend on:
Do modularization next - it's the right call and the timing is now ideal. The roadmap explicitly sequences #1 after a round of arithmetic work "so you modularize code you understand cold," and #2 was named the last feature to add to the monolith before modularizing. You've now finished #2. drill.py is ~3,400 lines and index.html ~2,400; every idea on your list widens that surface further. Splitting drill.py into config/db/logic/http/main and the frontend into ES modules is unusually low-risk here because the section boundaries are already strict and one-way, and the 282-test suite makes it fearless. It serves your build-to-learn goal directly (how concerns separate) and is invisible to a user - which is exactly why it should go before the fun user-facing drills, not be perpetually deferred behind them.
Then your "cool" ideas sort cleanly into three buckets:

Cheap, reuse existing infrastructure (do these first among the new drills): trivia (#23) and vocabulary/word drills are importers into the bank/question structure you already have - closest to "free," no new input mode. SM2 (#6) and user cards/items are the spaced-repetition pair, but the roadmap is firm that SM2 must come after adaptive selection (#7) because SM2 is a selection policy that plugs into the pick_next_question seam - doing SM2 first means building the seam twice. So the order there is #7  #6, and the v3 migration groundwork (ADR-025/040 reservations) is already laid for it.
Language drills - split by input mode. The alphabet/romanization and show-a-word-I-try-it-then-click-next flows you described are genuinely cheap: they're the existing text/MC/"reveal then self-grade" structure (the roadmap has alphabet at #21, and your "click next" idea is essentially a self-graded reveal card, which is even simpler than MC). Audio recording / pronunciation assessment is the expensive outlier - it's a true new input mode requiring MediaRecorder + storage, and actual pronunciation scoring via SpeechRecognition is already deferred at the bottom of your roadmap (ADR-006, #35) because assessment is hard. Your own instinct ("I can just drill for this, show and I try them and click next") is the right scope: ship the show/self-grade version, skip the recording/scoring entirely. That keeps it in the cheap bucket.
Maps - defer or rescope. "Show a map, light a region, name the country" is geography-visual (#26, Tier 4) - a real new input/render mode (SVG map, clickable regions). The self-graded version (light a region, user says it aloud, clicks to reveal + self-grade) collapses it into the same cheap reveal-card structure as the language drill, and the "maybe this is part of something else" you sensed is exactly that: it's a reveal-card drill with a map as the prompt medium. If you want maps, do them as a reveal-card variant after modularization, not as a bespoke mode.

So my concrete recommended path: D-UI-1  modularization (#1)  study curriculum (#3) as the learning capstone on the now-modular code  then breadth, cheapest-first: reveal-card drills (alphabet/words, and maps-as-reveal-card if you want them) and trivia importers, which need almost no new infrastructure  then the #7  #6 selection/SM2 pair when you want the adaptive/spaced-repetition depth. The one thing I'd not do early is audio recording or pronunciation scoring - your roadmap and your own framing both point to drilling pronunciation via self-graded reveal instead.
