tags: right-of-way, intersections
At an unmarked 4-way stop, four cars arrive at the same time.
In what order do they proceed, and what is the governing rule?
criteria: Yield to the vehicle on your right. All four drivers apply the same rule simultaneously, each yielding right until one car has no vehicle to its right that is also waiting.

@@@ id: drive-speed-school
tags: speed-limits, school-zones
What is the standard speed limit inside a school zone when children
are present, and what two conditions trigger the reduced limit?
criteria: 20 mph (may vary by state, but 20 is the standard default). Triggered when: (1) school zone signs and flashers are active, or (2) children are visibly present in or near the roadway.

@@@ id: drive-sign-solid-yellow
tags: lane-markings, centerline
A solid double yellow line runs down the center of a two-lane road.
What does it prohibit, and is there any condition under which crossing
it is permitted?
criteria: Prohibits passing in either direction. Crossing is permitted only to turn left into a driveway or intersection, or to avoid an obstruction, never to pass.

@@@ id: drive-lane-dotted-white
tags: lane-markings, lane-changes
What does a dashed white line between lanes on a multi-lane road
indicate, and what action does it permit?
criteria: Indicates lane separation in same-direction traffic. Drivers may change lanes when it is safe to do so. No prohibition on crossing.

@@@ id: drive-emergency-breakdown
tags: emergency, breakdown, highway
Your vehicle loses power on a highway. List the correct sequence
of actions from the moment you realize the car is failing.
criteria: (1) Signal and steer to the right shoulder. (2) Get as far off the travel lanes as possible. (3) Turn on hazard lights immediately. (4) Stay in the vehicle if safe; exit via right-side doors only if necessary. (5) Call for help; place warning triangles if available.
